_xanu. 7/14/2026 11:14 AM
Hey just a little update from what i've been working on in my branch to get you up to speed The first thing i did branching off version 1 was modify the sideplates. i learned a technique in mastersketching called layering where you layer the sketch and the layers are separated by how much the dimensions drive the entire part. your initial mastersketch is a great example of what would be a layer 1 mastersketch because almost everything relies or refrences it and then i added layer 2 sketches which get the shape and holes. basically what you sketched when designing the part inside the part studio i just moved that sketch to inside the plates mastersketch. that leads me to the next thing i did was separate the mastersketch of each plate. they both rely and refrence the same base layer1 sketch you made (though i ended up modifying it to make it longer to 17.5 inches since that seems to be the module length of the drivebase Mr. Shepard gave us in tinkercad). theres a new folder of master sketches and additionally from your inital sketch there is now an additional outer mastersketch and inner plate mastersketch. those respectively have their own individual layer 2 sketches. this allows them to be diven by the same base dimensions while also allowing slight customization and cleans up the part studio of the actual plate a little bit. in the part studios for each plate also additionally have a "pocketing sketch" which is because the way i lighten parts uses the feature called Vent and it requires faces from the sketch to remove rather than genrating and removing weight automatically. depite how they look it took way longer to make them since when making the lines, i looked for where the plate might encouter stress. so the lines directly support areas that might undergo stress. also for the middle part i tried to center the entire odometry pod assembly so i put the mount hole to the side. that was hard to make look good so i mirrored it on each side so either way mounts.
